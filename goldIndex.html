A Sublime Shimmer of Gold

From commencement remarks delivered before the Stanford University American Studies Class of 2013, 06.13.2013

trembling are the desert winds
as they sweep plaintively
from Indio to New Mexico
carrying with them tumbleweeds
birds of prey
and the ineffable life-force of the wide-open,
the quixotic in all of that grand nothingness.

"baby," he said, "I'm gonna find me a life"
way out west."(1)
"do I pray?" "do I play?"(2) "away."

intrepid, he set out in the footsteps of Machado
breaking dusty ground
tugging on bootstraps as he went
bearing chest, heart, and soul, to the wind
to the sun's implacable glower

the sun, a glowing ornament
a fixture in the heavens
cracking the parched desert floor
(thirsty for at least a thousand years)
and his weathered lips and mind.

when the rattler appeared on his third day
he gazed toward the distant horizon
and then at the mighty heavens above
dreaming of salty chicken noodle soup
the soft caress of his girl's bosom
a swig of Kentucky's finest, the nectar of beam
the whimsical notes of his banjo back home.

his wistful longings were but fleeting
floating east absent a care
succumbing to a splash of the sublime
to the divine radiance of fire gracing the horizon,
exploding riotously, liberally, in beams
of orange, pink, crimson, and yellow

a sublime shimmer of gold,
heralding the stars
inviting those brilliant bodies to twinkle
to dance playfully across the enveloping dark of night.

solace, he found
solace, in the crackling of a campfire
an offering from those feisty tumbleweeds
a saving grace from moonlit shivers
from the intermittent chill of solitude

and so he warmed his heart and his bones,
pioneering the extreme
and the extremes.

**

(1) Lord Huron, "Time to Run," Lonesome Dreams.

(2) Reference to American naturalist John Muir: "Everybody needs beauty as well as bread, places to pray in and play in, where nature may heal and give strength to body and soul."
